# Market Research: Todo / Task Management App

## Executive Summary

The task management software market is valued at ~$4.1 billion (2024) and growing at 13.7% CAGR, with the to-do list app sub-segment at ~$1.4 billion. The market is dominated by Todoist (30-50M users), TickTick (50M+ downloads), Microsoft To Do (free with 365), and Things 3 (Apple-only). Despite dozens of competitors, significant user dissatisfaction persists around subscription fatigue, feature bloat, paywalled basics, sync reliability, and ecosystem lock-in. A solo-developer SaaS opportunity exists for a fast, beautiful, web-first todo app with a generous free tier, transparent pricing, and no ecosystem lock-in.

### Key Market Trends
1. **AI-Driven Features**: Smart prioritization, natural language input, automated task breakdown. 55% user adoption of AI features in task management.
2. **Cross-Platform Demand**: 70% mobile usage. Users expect seamless sync across devices.

5. **Hybrid Work**: Remote/hybrid work driving demand for collaborative but not overly complex tools.

## User Pain Points (Ranked by Frequency)

### HIGH frequency complaints:

**1. Subscription fatigue for simple tools**
Users question paying $4-8/month for a checklist app. Over 60% of consumers are cutting subscriptions. Viral Product Hunt thread: "Best To Do app that doesn't have a subscription model?" Things 3's one-time purchase model is cited as a key reason users switch to it.
- Platforms: Reddit, MetaFilter, Product Hunt, Trustpilot
- Sources: [Product Hunt Discussion](https://www.producthunt.com/ask/10910-best-to-do-app-that-doesn-t-have-a-subscription-model), [Subscription Fatigue Analysis](https://hulry.com/app-subscription-fatigue/)

**2. Feature bloat and overwhelming complexity**
A viral Hacker News post titled "I tried every todo app and ended up with a .txt file" (Aug 2025) captured the sentiment that productivity apps add friction rather than reducing it. The author tried Notion, Todoist, Things 3, OmniFocus, Asana, Trello, Any.do, and TickTick before returning to a plain text file. Notion users report 20+ hours learning and still being confused.
- Platforms: Hacker News, Reddit, Medium, Lobsters
- Sources: [HN Discussion](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=44864134), [Original Article](https://www.al3rez.com/todo-txt-journey)

**3. Essential features paywalled**
Todoist locks reminders behind Pro ($4/mo). Calendar view is premium-only. Free plans limited to 5 projects (Todoist) or 9 lists (TickTick). Any.do locks recurring reminders behind premium. Users feel nickel-and-dimed for features they consider standard.
- Platforms: Reddit, Trustpilot, G2, Capterra
- Sources: [Todoist Pricing](https://www.todoist.com/pricing), [Todoist Free vs Pro Analysis](https://baizaar.tools/todoist-pro-vs-free-2025/)

### MEDIUM frequency complaints:

**4. Data loss and sync failures**
Microsoft To Do users report catastrophic data loss (200+ tasks vanishing). Todoist's Google Calendar 2-way sync is unreliable under heavy use. Trust in a task manager is foundational -- losing tasks destroys confidence.
- Sources: [MS To Do Data Loss](https://learn.microsoft.com/en-us/answers/questions/5538899/all-tasks-(to-do)-at-a-certain-point-are-gone-sinc)

**5. Calendar integration as an afterthought**
Most apps bolt on calendar as secondary feature. Todoist has no built-in calendar despite years of requests. Google Tasks only supports date (not time) deadlines.
- Sources: [Calendar Integration Analysis](https://fhynix.com/best-todo-app-with-tasks-and-calendar/)

**6. Platform and ecosystem lock-in**
Things 3 is Apple-only. MS To Do and Google Tasks tied to their respective ecosystems. Switching platforms means losing your task system.
- Sources: [Things 3 Platform Limitations](https://thedigitalprojectmanager.com/tools/things-3-review/)

**7. Overdue task guilt**
Todoist's overdue items cluttering the Today view creates anxiety. No app offers graceful rescheduling workflows for missed deadlines.
- Sources: [TickTick vs Todoist Review](https://rambox.app/blog/ticktick-vs-todoist/)

**8. Privacy and data ownership concerns**
42% of users have data privacy concerns. Growing movement toward open-source/self-hosted alternatives. Users report spam from Todoist that they cannot unsubscribe from.
- Sources: [Open Source Alternatives](https://super-productivity.com/blog/open-source-productivity-apps-comparison/)

## Feature Gaps and Opportunities

### Gap 1: No app balances simplicity with power at a fair price
**Evidence**: The "txt file" phenomenon shows users fleeing complexity. But simple free apps (Google Tasks, MS To Do) lack features. No app is both genuinely simple AND feature-complete without a paywall on basics.
**Opportunity**: Progressive disclosure -- fast and minimal by default, with power features discoverable but not in your face. Generous free tier including reminders and calendar view.

### Gap 2: First-class calendar + task unified view
**Evidence**: Todoist has no built-in calendar. Google Tasks is date-only. TickTick has calendar but is cluttered. Users consistently request unified task-calendar views.
**Opportunity**: Build calendar as a core feature, not a bolt-on. Show tasks on a timeline natively. Allow time-blocking without external sync.

### Gap 3: Graceful overdue task handling
**Evidence**: Todoist's overdue items clutter Today view. Users report growing anxiety from overdue task counts. No app offers smart rescheduling.
**Opportunity**: Build an "overdue triage" mode. When tasks pile up, offer review mode to reschedule, drop, or delegate. Frame missed deadlines as rescheduling opportunities.

### Gap 4: Cross-platform with no ecosystem lock-in
**Evidence**: Things 3 is Apple-only. MS To Do tied to Microsoft. Google Tasks tied to Google.
**Opportunity**: Web-first PWA that works beautifully on any device. No platform dependency. Export data anytime.

### Gap 5: Transparent pricing without paywalling basics
**Evidence**: Subscription fatigue is documented. Users want one-time or very affordable pricing. 60%+ cutting subscriptions.
**Opportunity**: Very low pricing ($2-3/month), lifetime deal option, or generous free tier. Gate AI and advanced analytics, not reminders and themes.

### Gap 6: Data ownership and portability
**Evidence**: EU Data Act mandates portability. Growing open-source movement. Privacy concerns at 42%.
**Opportunity**: Standard format exports (JSON, CSV). Transparent data practices. Optional local-first storage.

### Gap 7: Reflection and journaling
**Evidence**: Users request journal/reflection features. No major todo app offers daily review or qualitative reflection.
**Opportunity**: Optional daily/weekly reflection prompts tied to completed tasks.

### Gap 8: Lightweight collaboration without project management overhead
**Evidence**: Things 3 = zero collab. Google Tasks = zero collab. But Todoist/Asana push toward full PM tools.
**Opportunity**: Simple list sharing via link. Real-time sync for shared lists. No admin roles, no boards -- just "share this list."

### Market Pricing Landscape

| App | Free | Paid (Annual) | Paid (Monthly) | Model |
|-----|------|---------------|----------------|-------|
| Todoist | Yes (limited) | $4/mo | $5/mo | Per-user SaaS |

| Any.do | Yes (limited) | $4.99/mo | $7.99/mo | Per-user SaaS |
| Things 3 | No | $80 one-time | N/A | One-time purchase |
| MS To Do | Full | Free | Free | Bundled |
| Google Tasks | Full | Free | Free | Bundled |

### Features Commonly Gated Behind Paywalls
- Reminders and recurring reminders (Todoist, Any.do)
- Calendar views (Todoist)
- Custom filters and smart lists (Todoist, TickTick)
- Themes and customization (Any.do, TickTick)
- File attachment size/count limits (Todoist, TickTick)
- Project/list count limits (Todoist, TickTick)
- Statistics and analytics (TickTick)
- Location-based reminders (Any.do)
- Task duration tracking (TickTick)

### Pricing Recommendation for Solo-Dev SaaS
- **Free tier**: Generous -- include reminders, basic calendar view, up to 20 projects, themes. More generous than Todoist's free plan.
- **Premium tier**: $3/month annual ($36/year) or $4/month monthly. Undercut Todoist, match TickTick.
- **Lifetime deal**: Consider $49-79 one-time (addresses subscription fatigue, competes with Things 3's model on all platforms).
- **What to gate**: AI features, advanced analytics/statistics, priority support, increased storage. NOT reminders, NOT calendar view, NOT themes.

## Recommended Positioning

### Target Audience
Individual professionals and small households who have tried the major todo apps and found them either too complex (Todoist, TickTick, Notion) or too basic (Google Tasks, MS To Do). People who value design and speed but refuse to pay $5/month for a reminder feature.

### Value Proposition
**"A fast, beautiful todo app that gives you everything you need for free -- and never makes you feel guilty about overdue tasks."**

### Key Differentiators (for a solo-dev product)
1. **Generous free tier** -- reminders, calendar view, and reasonable limits included free (unlike Todoist/TickTick)
2. **Web-first, cross-platform** -- works on any device with a browser (unlike Things 3, unlike ecosystem-locked apps)
3. **Fast and minimal by default** -- progressive disclosure of power features (unlike TickTick's feature overload)
4. **Beautiful design** -- Things 3 quality aesthetics accessible on every platform
5. **Fair, transparent pricing** -- low subscription with optional lifetime deal
6. **Data portability** -- export anytime in standard formats, no lock-in
7. **Graceful overdue handling** -- rescheduling workflows instead of guilt-inducing overdue counts

### Anti-Positioning (what this is NOT)
- NOT a project management tool (no Gantt charts, sprint boards, or resource allocation)
- NOT an enterprise collaboration platform (no admin dashboards or compliance features)
- NOT a Notion competitor (no databases, wikis, or knowledge management)
- NOT trying to replace your calendar app (complements it, doesn't replace it)

### Competitive Moat for Solo Developer
- **Speed**: Ship fast, iterate based on direct user feedback. Large competitors are slow (MS To Do has years-old feature requests; Things 3 updates infrequently).
- **Niche focus**: Do personal task management exceptionally well instead of expanding into project management.
- **Community**: Build in public, engage directly with users. Small teams at indie scale create loyalty that VC-funded companies cannot replicate.
- **Design taste**: One developer with strong design sensibility can out-design a committee-driven product.
